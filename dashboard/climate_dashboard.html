Key Findings & Societal Impact
🌧️
1-Month Rainfall Lag on Dengue
Rainfall affects Dengue cases with a 1-month biological delay (corr = 0.513) — matching real-world mosquito breeding cycles. This enables early warning systems.
🤖
Random Forest: 99.2% Accuracy
RF achieves R² = 0.992 vs Linear Regression's 0.866 — proving ML captures non-linear climate-disease relationships that traditional models miss.
📅
Seasonality Drives Disease Most
Month encoding accounts for 91.9% of feature importance — confirming disease is strongly seasonal, with monsoon and post-monsoon as peak risk periods.
🧮
5 PCA Components Explain 95%
Dimensionality reduced from 9 variables to 5 components while retaining 95.49% of variance — PC1 (52.5%) driven by seasonal climate patterns.
🌡️
Hot & Dry = Highest Risk
Scenario analysis shows a Hot & Dry climate produces the highest dengue case predictions (124 cases/month) — critical for public health planning.
🏥
Supports SDG 3 & SDG 13
The framework directly supports Good Health & Wellbeing (SDG 3) and Climate Action (SDG 13) by linking climate data to actionable disease insights.
